Graduation Project - AN ONTOLOGY-BASED APPROACH FOR ACTIVITY RECOGNITION FROM SENSORS IN OFFICES

Understanding people behaviors in a building is useful to save energy of buildings and improve efficiency of employees. To study the behaviors of people in offices, we use sensors to collect raw data about people moving throughout buildings. To understand the massive amounts of sensor data, we develop an ontology, written in Common Logic, that describes the information at a higher level. Then we translate the ontology to MySQL scripts and algorithms. With these programs, we can retrieve some important information about offices such as: how frequently are offices and shares spaces utilized? How many meetings take place on average during a week? A number of use cases demonstrate the capabilities of the system.
